1. Safety & Customer-Supplied Parts

We may refuse installation of any part that could compromise safe, reliable operation. Please do not stand over or interfere with the workspace during service.

2. Payments & Refunds

  • Payment in full is due upon completion. Stopping a job does not cancel labor charges.
  • Accepted: Cash, PayPal, credit/debit cards. Electronic payments may include a ~3% processing fee.
  • Refunds require proof from a licensed mechanic and a verifiable printed receipt.
  • If service must pause due to parts/external issues, half the labor is due that day; the balance is due upon completion.
↑ Back to top

3. Parts, Pickup & Markup

Technician pickup fee: $10 (within 10 miles) or $30 (20+ miles). Customer delays beyond 30 minutes: $50 per half-hour unless communicated.

If we purchase parts, a market-based markup applies for sourcing, transport, and warranty handling.

Parts Deposit Policy: If Vehicle Tips LLC purchases parts on your behalf, a 50% deposit of the total parts and labor is required before parts are ordered. The balance is due upon job completion. Deposits are non-refundable if the customer cancels after parts have been purchased.

If customers supply their own parts, Vehicle Tips LLC does not warranty them, and new labor fees apply if those parts fail.

5. Cancellations & Refunds

Reschedule or cancel at least 72 hours before the appointment. Cancellations within 72 hours are non-refundable (including diagnostics, maintenance, inspections, A/C recharges, and virtual sessions).

If parts were ordered through Vehicle Tips LLC and you cancel, you are responsible for the parts cost + applicable markup and any Service Call / Trip Fee already incurred.

6. Bathroom Breaks

As mobile mechanics, we may briefly leave to use a nearby public restroom. We do not ask to use a customer’s home restroom. If we step away, we’ll notify you by text/call/email and secure the unfinished job site before leaving.

7. Safety Perimeter at Job Sites

For everyone’s safety, we create a marked work zone using hazard cones and plastic chain. Please ensure children, pets, and bystanders remain outside this perimeter until the area is cleared.

↑ Back to top

8. Pre-Work Photo Documentation & Inspection

Before starting work, we take photos and complete a multi-point inspection form. We also note site conditions (e.g., existing debris or oil stains). This protects both the customer and the technician by keeping a clear record of the vehicle and the workspace.
