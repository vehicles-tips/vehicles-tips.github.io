Steering & Suspension Maintenance Tips

Keep your vehicle's steering and suspension in top shape for safety, comfort, and smooth driving.

A properly maintained steering and suspension system ensures safe handling, smooth rides, and longer tire life. Ignoring these components can lead to poor control, uneven tire wear, and costly repairs.

1. Check Shock Absorbers and Struts

Inspect shocks and struts for leaks, dents, or corrosion. Worn shocks reduce ride comfort and increase stopping distance. Replace them if performance has noticeably declined.

2. Inspect Steering Components

Regularly check tie rods, ball joints, and steering linkage for wear or looseness. Any play in steering components can affect handling and lead to unsafe driving conditions.

3. Maintain Proper Wheel Alignment

Misaligned wheels cause uneven tire wear and pull your vehicle to one side. Have your alignment checked periodically or after hitting curbs or potholes.

4. Examine Suspension Bushings

Suspension bushings absorb shock and reduce vibration. Cracked or worn bushings can cause clunks, vibrations, and rough handling. Replace damaged bushings promptly.

5. Monitor Tire Wear and Pressure

Uneven tire wear can indicate suspension or alignment issues. Maintain proper tire pressure and rotate tires as recommended to ensure even wear.

6. Schedule Professional Inspections

Even with routine checks, a certified mechanic should inspect your steering and suspension annually to ensure safety and optimal performance.
