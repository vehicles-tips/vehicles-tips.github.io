5 Tips to Maintain Your Brakes

Simple brake maintenance tips to improve safety, extend component life, and avoid costly repairs.

Your braking system is one of the most critical safety components on your vehicle. Regular maintenance helps prevent sudden failures, improves stopping power, and reduces long-term repair costs.

1. Inspect Brake Pads Regularly

Brake pads wear down over time and should be inspected at least once a month. Replace them before they reach minimum thickness to avoid damaging rotors.

2. Monitor Brake Fluid Condition

Brake fluid transfers force from the pedal to the brakes. Low or contaminated fluid can reduce braking efficiency and cause system failure.

3. Pay Attention to Unusual Noises

Squealing, grinding, or vibration when braking often signals worn pads or rotors. Addressing these early prevents more expensive repairs.

4. Avoid Excessive Vehicle Weight

Carrying excessive cargo puts extra strain on your brakes and suspension. Staying within recommended weight limits helps extend brake life.

5. Schedule Professional Brake Inspections

Even if no symptoms are present, having a professional inspect your brakes annually ensures optimal performance and safety.
